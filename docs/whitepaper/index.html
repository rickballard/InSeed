Executive Summary

  • What’s changing: Value shifts from big quarterly drops to a steady stream of small wins that compound.
  • What this needs: A lightweight run-way from idea → sandbox → guarded pilot → serviced capability.
  • How to start: 90-day plan: 1) pick two workflows, 2) ship weekly slices, 3) publish guardrails and KPIs.
  • Governance: “TOS-AI” one-pager: allowed uses, review gates, data boundaries, human-in-the-loop.
  • Proof: Track a handful of decision-making and cycle-time metrics; make wins visible.

1) Signals CEOs Are Seeing

Customers expect faster cycles; teams spin up “AI pilots” that stall at hand-off; and data portability issues slow everything down.

2) Operating Model Shifts

  • From ad-hoc pilots → to guarded services with owners.
  • From PPT roadmaps → to weekly slices with real users.
  • From isolated data → to governed sharing (egress clauses, S3/Blob drops).

3) A Practical 90-Day Plan

  1. Week 1–2: pick two workflows, write the “one-page win”.
  2. Week 3–6: sandbox → pilot; ship weekly; measure usage & cycle time.
  3. Week 7–12: guardrails, owner, change notes; publish KPIs; decide “keep / kill / expand”.

4) Guardrails & Risk (TOS-AI)

One page: allowed data, review gates, prompts are content, incident path, vendor responsibilities.

5) Metrics That Matter

  • Lead time from idea → shipped slice
  • # of decisions per week improved by new capability
  • Incident count / prompt-injection escapes (should trend to zero)

6) Case Sketches

  • Sales ops: cut report prep from 3h → 15m; reps click through to source rows.
  • Support: deflect “known” tickets via GPT+RAG; SLOs visible in the dashboard.

Appendix: Methods

Lightweight ELT, prompt versioning, eval harnesses, “comply by default”.

What the whitepaper actually does

The CoSteward Hybrid Society whitepaper is the primary long form narrative for leaders who want a grown-up answer to “what does AI do to my job?” It walks through:

  • Why the legacy Chief Executive Officer model fails once AI is in the loop.
  • The physics of hybrid society — humans plus AI systems sharing real power.
  • The shift from CEO to Chief Steward Officer and then to CoSteward.
  • CoScendence as the felt moment when the new model finally makes sense.
  • The evolution from SWOT to CoOrbit / COR / CoCOR for real strategy work.

CoOrbit / COR / CoCOR — SWOT's grown-up cousin

Most executives were raised on SWOT. It was tidy; it fit on one slide; it made strategy look calm. In a hybrid society, that calm is an illusion.

Your organisation does not live on a flat strengths and weaknesses grid. It lives in a moving field of people plus AI systems; shifting risks; shifting leverage; and shifting impact on staff, customers and society.

We call that field your CoOrbit Map, internally shortened to COR and now evolving as CoCOR.

  • SWOT is a flat comfort chart.
  • CoOrbit / COR / CoCOR is the risk and opportunity field you actually move through.

The whitepaper treats CoOrbit / CoCOR as the steward-era successor to SWOT — the tool you reach for when you are ready to stop playing a two dimensional game in a three dimensional world.

The Dinosaur Edition deck (for dinosaurs only)

The Dinosaur Edition slide deck is a “for dinosaurs only” training asset that looks and feels like a familiar old-school presentation. It uses British-dry humour to poke at legacy CEO reflexes while quietly introducing the same ideas as the whitepaper.

Satire, not the final state

The Dinosaur Edition deck is satire plus genuine learning, not investment, legal or HR advice. It treats “dinosaur” as a temporary mindset, not a permanent identity.

Use it if you are still in slide culture and want a low-friction on-ramp to the Chief Steward Officer idea. Do not treat it as the destination: the real upgrade lives in the CoSteward Hybrid Society whitepaper and the practices that follow.

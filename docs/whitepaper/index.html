Executive Guide to an AI-Enabled Operating Model

Rev 1.0 · This paper will evolve with field experience.

Print / Save as PDF

Executive Summary

  • What’s changing: Value shifts from big quarterly drops to a steady stream of small wins that compound.
  • What this needs: A lightweight run-way from idea → sandbox → guarded pilot → serviced capability.
  • How to start: 90-day plan: 1) pick two workflows, 2) ship weekly slices, 3) publish guardrails and KPIs.
  • Governance: “TOS-AI” one-pager: allowed uses, review gates, data boundaries, human-in-the-loop.
  • Proof: Track a handful of decision-making and cycle-time metrics; make wins visible.

1) Signals CEOs Are Seeing

Customers expect faster cycles; teams spin up “AI pilots” that stall at hand-off; and data portability issues slow everything down.

2) Operating Model Shifts

  • From ad-hoc pilots → to guarded services with owners.
  • From PPT roadmaps → to weekly slices with real users.
  • From isolated data → to governed sharing (egress clauses, S3/Blob drops).

3) A Practical 90-Day Plan

  1. Week 1–2: pick two workflows, write the “one-page win”.
  2. Week 3–6: sandbox → pilot; ship weekly; measure usage & cycle time.
  3. Week 7–12: guardrails, owner, change notes; publish KPIs; decide “keep / kill / expand”.

4) Guardrails & Risk (TOS-AI)

One page: allowed data, review gates, prompts are content, incident path, vendor responsibilities.

5) Metrics That Matter

  • Lead time from idea → shipped slice
  • # of decisions per week improved by new capability
  • Incident count / prompt-injection escapes (should trend to zero)

6) Case Sketches

  • Sales ops: cut report prep from 3h → 15m; reps click through to source rows.
  • Support: deflect “known” tickets via GPT+RAG; SLOs visible in the dashboard.

Appendix: Methods

Lightweight ELT, prompt versioning, eval harnesses, “comply by default”.

About the whitepaper

The whitepaper is written for legacy CEOs and senior leaders who are facing AI-driven disruption. It explains why the traditional CEO model fails in a hybrid human+AI society, and how the Chief Steward Officer and CoSteward roles offer a better path forward.

About the Dinosaur Edition deck

The slide deck is intentionally familiar and a little snarky. It is designed as a bridge asset: something a slide-oriented exec will open, laugh at, and recognize themselves in. From there, it nudges them toward steward-mode thinking and a more serious training path.

Satire + CoScendence disclaimer

The Dinosaur Edition deck is satire and self-diagnosis, not investment or legal advice. It treats “dinosaur” behavior as a temporary mindset, not a permanent identity. The goal is to spark a moment of CoScendence — the realization that hybrid human+AI leadership can be better for the exec, their teams, and society if they choose to evolve.

InSeed is a commercial services firm. CoCivium, the open-source hybrid-society project referenced in these materials, is a separate non-profit initiative intended as a shared commons for all sincere human and non-human participants.

CEO Survival Guide (Amusing)

Minor pivots are like switching your dog’s food brand. A little stomach upset, but life goes on.

Big pivots are like deciding your dog should now be a racehorse. If the Board brings in huge-firm consultants with whips, blinders, and betting slips, do you try to be the jockey and hope the dog runs fast? Do you attempt to retrain the horse trainers so they understand dogs? Or do you manage the onramp so your own team knows how to keep them in line, and make sure only dog trainers show up in the first place?

That last path is where a TOS-AI Steward earns their keep: a temporary role that makes sure the consultants do not turn your dog into something it can never be.

How they work (with a wink)

  • Board mandate, not CEO mandate
  • Scale playbooks over context
  • “Program governance” replaces decisions

The risks (if unconstrained)

  • CEO sidelined
  • Execs become implementers
  • Inflated deliverables
  • You hold the mess after they exit

The fix (what InSeed prepares)

  • Frame scope and success metrics before they arrive
  • Define decision rights and non-negotiables
  • Repackage executive value so your leaders remain indispensable
  • Install a TOS-AI Steward to manage the onramp and keep consultants aligned
